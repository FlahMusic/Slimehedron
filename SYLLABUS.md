# slimehedron learn — scope & sequence

*One page for teachers, parents, and anyone evaluating the program.
Free. No accounts. No ads. Works offline. All vocabulary is real music terminology.*

## The path

| # | Course | The concept | The activity | Mastery signal (never a grade) |
|---|--------|-------------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| 1 | **rhythm** | steady beat, tempo | tap the pad with the tick; feel fast vs slow | 8 on-beat taps fill the dots |
| 2 | **the piano** | pitch space, note names, octave repetition | find each key by its landmark (2-and-3 black-key groups) | all 7 names found |
| 3 | **intervals** | distance between notes | hear two notes, choose the distance; slimes stand the distance apart on the keys | correct picks accumulate |
| 4 | **chords** | 3rds stacking; maj/min/sus/dim/aug/7ths | build C-chords on the piano; hear one note move and change the mood | all 9 chord types visited |
| 5 | **the seven modes** *(capstone)* | mode = mood; scale structure | scene-matching ear training + composing over mode backings with an in-key piano | 7 mode stickers + hidden-slime cameos |

## Design principles (research-backed)

- **Pulse before pitch, sound before symbol** — Kodály sequence; Gordon's Music
  Learning Theory (audiation first).
- **Spiral curriculum** — each course revisits listening at greater depth (Bruner 1960).
- **Multimodal**: every concept is seen (scenes, colored keys), heard (live synth),
  and done (tapping, key-finding, composing) — Mayer's multimedia principle.
- **No wrong answers, no locks** — unassessed exploration sustains engagement;
  progress dots use the goal-gradient effect without grading.
- **Word economy** — real terms (quantize, envelope, dorian), simplest possible
  sentences around them; built to machine-translate cleanly.

## Standards alignment (National Core Arts Standards anchors)

- **Creating** — compose mode: children generate melodic ideas over harmonic backings.
- **Performing** — rhythm pad and pixel piano are performance surfaces with instant feedback.
- **Responding** — scene-matching ear training is structured responding to musical mood.
- **Connecting** — modes tied to visual scenes and public-domain repertoire
  (Beethoven, traditional folk, Sakura) connects music to culture and history.

## Beyond the path

**Play mode** is open-ended creation (the physics instrument, full band, recording).
**Studio mode** is the ceiling: synthesis (oscillators, ADSR, filters), effects,
tempo-synced delay, MIDI export of every part — the same vocabulary as a real studio,
so nothing learned here has to be unlearned later.
