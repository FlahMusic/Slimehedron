# Slimehedron Learn Mode: Teaching Music Modes to Kids

## Executive Summary

This guide designs a free, **no-assessment** music theory learn mode for Slimehedron that teaches the seven musical modes through visual-scene matching and interactive creation. Research shows this combination works: Chrome Music Lab reached 60 million users and transformed music education by removing barriers (no login, no theory prerequisite, instant feedback). We apply the same principles to modes—a topic no major free tool teaches to children yet.

## Why This Approach Works: The Research

### 1. Multimodal Learning Beats Single Modality
Research from Cisco found students who received text + visuals learned better than text-only; students prefer visual inputs in lessons. For modes specifically:

- **Visual:** Pixel art scenes (cute slime mood)
- **Auditory:** Slimehedron synth playing the mode over a chordal backdrop
- **Kinesthetic:** Kids compose their own melody in the mode using the synth + pixel piano

Music + movement pedagogy shows 59% of preschoolers achieved high knowledge levels with a 0.93 effectiveness parameter using Kodály methods (multi-sensory approach).

### 2. Visual-First Learning Works for Modes
Elementary music educators successfully use visual representations (colored cards, floor tiles) to teach scales; Dr. Edwin Gordon's Music Learning Theory research shows children exposed to movement, listening, and singing develop stronger understanding than single-modality learners.

For modes: each scene IS the visual anchor. Kids see it first, hear it second, create with it third.

### 3. No Wrong Answers = Higher Engagement
In digital music tools, unassessed learning processes are more favored by students; multimodal combinations improve cognitive processing and learning efficiency. Chrome Music Lab was adopted across elementary to high school because it requires zero setup, no prior knowledge, and teachers treat it like a Swiss Army knife for lesson planning.

**Key**: Kids engage longer when there's no "correct answer" pressure. The create mode should celebrate *all* choices.

### 4. Chrome Music Lab's Winning Formula (Proven at Scale)
Chrome Music Lab's success comes from: free access, no login, instant-to-use, device-agnostic (browser on any device), built with open web tech, no account required. Families love it because it's 100% free, browser-based, instantly playful, and kids can experiment with no account or prior music theory. Teachers built entire lesson ecosystems on top of it.

## Learn Mode Structure

### Overview
Two complementary modes (pun intended):
1. **Guess Mode** (Ear Training) — hear a jam, pick which scene/mode it matches
2. **Create Mode** (No Wrong Answers) — see a scene, score it yourself with the slime synth

Both feed into one state: the seven diatonic modes + Ionian (major scale) = 8 total scenes.

## The Seven Scenes (Pixel Art + Hidden Slime Cameos)

Each scene is hand-designed, ~100-150px square, in cute pastel slime aesthetic. Each has a unique vibe that matches the mode's sonic character. The slime cameo is a small, easy-to-spot detail (eye, smile, blob shape) hidden in each.

### Scene Designs & Mode Pairings

**1. Ionian (Major Scale) — "Sunny Meadow"**
- Bright green grass, blue sky, single sun in corner
- Vibes: Happy, bright, resolute
- Audio: Ionian jam (CEG harmony, mostly C major scale)
- Slime: Small grinning slime blob in grass corner
- Why kids relate: "It's a happy day; I feel good"

**2. Dorian (i, iv, v vibes) — "Twilight Forest"**
- Darker green trees, purple sky, single moon
- Vibes: Mysterious, moody, slightly jazzy
- Audio: Dorian jam (D minor over C major, D-G-A harmony)
- Slime: Glowing eyes peeking from behind tree
- Why kids relate: "It's getting dark; something cool is about to happen"

**3. Phrygian (Exotic, spicy) — "Desert Oasis"**
- Sand dunes, single palm tree, stars
- Vibes: Exotic, dramatic, intense
- Audio: Phrygian jam (E Phrygian over C major, lots of E-G-B harmony)
- Slime: Slime puddle reflecting starlight
- Why kids relate: "It's far away and mysterious; feels different"

**4. Lydian (Quirky, lifted) — "Floating Islands"**
- Island in sky, clouds, unusual color palette
- Vibes: Whimsical, lifted, bright but odd
- Audio: Lydian jam (F Lydian over C major, F-A-C with that raised 4th)
- Slime: Small slime blob hovering between islands
- Why kids relate: "It's weird and cool; upside-down world"

**5. Mixolydian (Bluesy, retro) — "Vintage Carnival"**
- Colorful tent, carousel shapes, warm colors
- Vibes: Groovy, soulful, retro, fun
- Audio: Mixolydian jam (G Mixolydian over C major, G-B-D with flat 7th)
- Slime: Slime forming spinning carousel horse shape
- Why kids relate: "Party time; fun and a bit retro"

**6. Aeolian (Natural Minor) — "Rainy Cityscape"**
- City silhouette, rain, night sky
- Vibes: Sad, introspective, dark
- Audio: Aeolian jam (A natural minor over C major, A-C-E harmony)
- Slime: Slime dripping down a rain-soaked window
- Why kids relate: "It's sad or thoughtful; I feel quiet"

**7. Locrian (Diminished, tense) — "Fractured Mirror"**
- Broken mirror/crystal shapes, cool color palette
- Vibes: Unstable, dissonant, eerie
- Audio: Locrian jam (B Locrian over C major, B-D-F with stacked tensions)
- Slime: Slime shattered into crystalline pieces
- Why kids relate: "Something's broken or wrong; feels uncomfortable (in a cool way)"

## Lesson Plan: Structured Progression

### Phase 1: Introduction (5 minutes)
**Goal:** Expose kids to all seven scenes + sounds without pressure.

**Activity:** Slideshow mode
- Show each scene for 3-5 seconds
- Play the associated mode jam underneath (no talking over it)
- Kid just observes and listens
- After all seven: **"You just heard seven different moods. Each one is a different MODE. Let's explore them more."**

No quiz, no "do you remember?" — just immersion.

### Phase 2: Guess Mode - Easy (10 minutes)
**Goal:** Train ear-to-scene matching.

**Setup:**
- Player hears a 4-8 bar jam in one mode
- Two scene options appear (e.g., "Sunny Meadow" vs "Twilight Forest")
- Kid picks which scene matches the sound
- Immediate positive feedback: "You got it! That's Dorian (Twilight Forest)" or "Nope, but listen again—this one has that mysterious vibe"

**Progression:**
- Rounds 1-3: 2 choices (easy discrimination)
- Rounds 4-6: 3 choices (medium)
- Rounds 7+: 4 choices or mix across all 7 (hard)

**Audio design:**
- Each jam is distinct in character (major ≠ minor ≠ exotic)
- Use different chord progressions, same synth voice
- No lyrics, no obvious tricks — pure listening

**Score:** Kid gets points for speed + accuracy. No deduction for wrong guesses (growth mindset).

### Phase 3: Create Mode - Free (15+ minutes)
**Goal:** Let kids compose in each mode without fear.

**The Pixel Piano Setup:**
- Horizontal row of 8-16 pixel keys (cute, pastel-colored)
- Keys are automatically filtered to the selected mode's scale degrees
- When you click a key, slimehedron synth plays that note
- Tempo/BPM is synced to a chord backing (stays in the mode)

**Workflow:**
1. Kid picks a scene (e.g., "Twilight Forest" = Dorian)
2. Backing track starts (D minor over C major, ~100 BPM, 8-bar loop, gentle drums)
3. Kid taps out a melody on the pixel piano
4. No "undo," no "wrong notes"—every note in the mode sounds good
5. After 30 seconds: **"That sounds great! Here's what a composer might do..."** (show a pre-recorded professional example in the same mode)
6. Kid can keep playing or try another scene

**Why This Works:**
- Open-ended exploration (no "right answer") works when you let students become "experts" in each experiment and share what they learned
- Every note is in-scale, so the kid always sounds good
- Backing track provides harmonic anchor
- Comparison to "pro example" is gentle, celebratory, not judgmental

**Optional: Recording & Sharing**
- Let kids save their 30-sec composition as a shareable link (à la Chrome Music Lab's Song Maker)
- No login required
- Can view/play others' creations (crowdsourced gallery)

## Game Mechanics & Engagement Hooks

### 1. Find the Slime Cameo
Each scene has a hidden slime detail. After playing Guess Mode correctly 3 times on a mode, unlock a "Cameo Reveal" button:
- Shows a zoomed, cropped detail of the slime
- Kid finds it in the full scene
- Triggers a cute animation ("You found me!")
- Builds collection/achievement without grading

### 2. Mode Mastery Badges (Optional)
- **Listener:** Guess a mode correctly 5 times
- **Composer:** Create a melody in each mode
- **Discoverer:** Find all 7 slime cameos
- No "locked behind" paywall—just visual recognition

### 3. The "Composer's Choice" Playlist
- After kid creates, ask: "What scene would you LIKE to hear next?"
- Pick the mode they choose (based on mood/preference, not skill)
- Reinforces agency: "You're in control"

### 4. Cross-Modal Challenge (Optional, for older kids 10+)
- Hear a short scene description (text): "It's a sad rainy day"
- Kid picks the scene that matches the mood
- Then picks the mode that matches that mood
- Bridges emotional understanding → musical structures

## Pedagogical Principles Baked In

### No Assessment, Only Celebration
- No "wrong answer" language
- Failed guesses: "Let's listen again—what do you hear?" (curiosity, not shame)
- Every creation is good: "That's YOUR version. Composers write different melodies all the time."

### Instant Feedback Loop
- Click = immediate sound/visual response
- No lag, no "submit and wait"
- Chrome Music Lab's power is the ability to hear your musical doodles come to life instantly, which is empowering

### Scaffolding
- Phase 1: Listen only (immersion)
- Phase 2: Recognize patterns (ear training)
- Phase 3: Create freely (ownership)
- Optional Phase 4: Understand theory (why modes work)

### Multisensory Input
- **Visual:** Pixel scenes, cute aesthetic
- **Auditory:** Mode jams, synth feedback, backing tracks
- **Kinesthetic:** Clicking keys, tapping rhythm
- Audiovisual stimulation enhanced both acquisition and retrieval in children under 12 vs. visual-only; multisensory gains are highest in younger kids
